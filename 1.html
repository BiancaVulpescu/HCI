OSC-AR: A Mixed-Reality Platform for Oscar Film Critique

Enhancing Film Evaluation with MR and Mobile Collaboration

Abstract

OSC-AR integrates a mixed-reality (MR) headset with a companion mobile application to streamline the film critique process for Oscar jurors. By providing subtle, context-sensitive overlays during screenings and a structured collaboration environment post-screening, OSC-AR enables thorough evaluation, organized note-taking, and asynchronous discussion among geographically distributed critics.

Introduction

Judging films for the Academy Awards requires deep artistic analysis, real-time insights, and collaborative deliberation. Traditional note-taking disrupts immersion, and scattered feedback hampers post-screening discussions. OSC-AR addresses these challenges by combining MR overlays with a robust mobile app for synchronized reviews and structured dialogue.

System Overview

The OSC-AR system consists of two primary components: an MR headset that delivers non-intrusive, scene-relevant annotations, and a mobile application that organizes notes, scores films using AI-assisted sentiment analysis, and facilitates asynchronous jury discussions.

Methodology

Literature Review

We conducted a comprehensive review of existing mixed-reality and film-critique platforms, examining peer-reviewed articles, industry white papers, and developer documentation to identify best practices for real-time annotation, note-taking workflows, and collaborative review interfaces.

Survey

An online survey was distributed to 57 professional film critics and jurors to assess:

  • Current pain points in film review workflows
  • Preferred features in MR and mobile tools
  • Frequency of note-taking interruptions during screenings

Responses were analyzed to inform OSC-AR’s design priorities and feature set.

Results

The online survey (n=57) of professional film critics and jurors yielded the following distributions:

How often do you take notes during a film screening?

  • Never (0%): 17.5%
  • Very rarely (≤25%): 26.3%
  • Occasionally (26–50%): 26.3%
  • Frequently (51–75%): 14.0%
  • Always (>75%): 15.8%

How useful do you find an MR interface that shows your notes and live statistics without distracting you?

  • Never useful: 19.3%
  • Rarely useful: 17.5%
  • Sometimes useful: 22.8%
  • Very useful: 15.8%
  • Essential: 24.6%

How disruptive do you find interrupting the screening to take notes?

  • Not at all disruptive: 24.6%
  • Slightly disruptive: 12.3%
  • Moderately disruptive: 26.3%
  • Very disruptive: 12.3%
  • Extremely disruptive: 24.6%
